TUBERCULOSIS

TUBERCULOSIS PRECAUTIONS:

Precautions for tuberculosis (TB) prevention involve screening those at high risk, early detection and treatment of cases, and vaccination with the bacillus Calmette-Guérin (BCG) vaccine. People with latent TB do not spread the disease, but those with weakened immune systems are more likely to progress to active disease. Preventive measures include practicing good hygiene when coughing, avoiding contact with others, and wearing a mask. In healthcare settings, special measures like respirators and well-ventilated spaces are important to reduce infection risk. WHO recommends rapid diagnostic tests as initial diagnostic tests in all persons with signs and symptoms of TB, which can provide initial results to guide treatment decisions within 48 hours of sample collection.
