CHOLESTEROL

CHOLESTEROL PRECAUTIONS:

Precautions for managing cholesterol levels include maintaining a healthy diet, regular exercise, and avoiding smoking. It is also important to manage other risk factors such as obesity, diabetes, and hypertension. Regular check-ups with a healthcare provider can help monitor cholesterol levels and adjust treatment as necessary The same heart-healthy lifestyle changes that can lower cholesterol also can help prevent high cholesterol. You can practice the following habits: Eat a diet that focuses on lean protein, fruits, vegetables and whole grains. Limit sodium and added sugar. Also limit the amount of saturated and trans fats you eat. Instead, eat foods with healthy fats such as fatty or oily fish, nuts, and olive or canola oil. Lose extra weight and keep it off. If you smoke, ask your care team to help you quit. Exercise on most days of the week for at least 30 minutes. Drink less alcohol, if at all. Limit alcohol to no more than up to one drink a day for women and up to two drinks a day for men.
